Stereo Matching
SSD block-matching: each pixel in the left image finds its best match in the right image by sliding a window. Larger disparity = closer to the camera.
Tip: Larger window sizes are smoother but lose detail at depth boundaries. Increase max disparity if your camera baseline is wide.
Feature Detection & Matching
SIFT keypoints are detected in both images, then matched with a brute-force matcher using Lowe's ratio test (threshold 0.75) to keep only reliable matches.
Fundamental Matrix F
Computed via the normalised 8-point algorithm. F encodes the epipolar geometry between the two views — any point x in image 1 satisfies x'ᵀFx = 0 with its correspondence x' in image 2.
Epipolar Lines
A point in one image constrains its correspondence to a line in the other image. Coloured dots and their matching epipolar lines are shown — they should intersect the corresponding point.

Stereo Rectification
Uncalibrated rectification warps both images so epipolar lines become horizontal scanlines. Stereo matching then becomes a 1-D search — faster and more accurate.
